About This Project

Project Overview

This project visualizes public transit services in Seattle using interactive web mapping. The goal is to help users explore transit routes, stop accessibility, and neighborhood-level transportation patterns across the city. By highlighting variations in walk-based access to public transportation across neighborhoods and provide an intuitive way, it is for users to compare the accessibility levels of different areas in Seattle to provide a clear, visual way for users to understand how public transportation connects different parts of Seattle and how it intersects with daily mobility and neighborhood dynamics. For this project, we decided to focus on creating an interactive smart dashboard about transportation accessibility in the Seattle area. From students to elders, the intended audience for our project includes individuals who rely on public transportation, those who commute, and those without access to a private vehicles.

Project Functions

This webpage is an interactive smart dashboard that visualizes public transit accessibility across Seattle. Users can select a neighborhood or view the entire city to see walking time access to the nearest bus stops and Link light rail stations, along with summary indicators such as coverage within a chosen walking time threshold and transportation density. The dashboard supports comparison between neighborhoods and helps identify areas with strong transit access versus areas that may be underserved.

Data Sources

Data used in this map include King County Metro transit stops (King County Washington), Neighborhood Map Atlas Neighborhoods boundaries (City of Seattle ArcGIS Online), and transit route datasets from the Seattle Transportation Plan (City of Seattle ArcGIS Online).
